INSPIRATION

In her opinion, finding inspiration is something that you learn. Most people believe that no idea can be original because in all these years, it must've already been thought of by someone else. Sarah was a firm believer of this concept aswell, until she spend some time around other artists. When you do this, you start to see the world differently. You learn to look at everything around you and notice things that others do not see. Simple things, like a single beam of light that enters through the window or the smallest corner that has been slit off a cupboard, can give you inspiration for a new work of art. Therefor, looking at other artist and their work can be one of the greatest forms of inspiration for a young mind. They teach you about the practical part of painting, about style and colour and also show you a way into their mindset.

Language and Art

Language is a very powerful tool. It gives expression to our experiences, it creates a way to meet with someone else, it can unconsciously shape our perception and reality. In this way, language can be a huge contribution to an artpiece. It could move or influence the spectator even more. Currently, Sarah is researching how to insert words and sentences on a painting to give her art that extra push. 

Below you can find some artists who ispire Sarah to incorporate language into her work.
