Sarah Lefèvre is a young Belgian painter. Her work is mainly impressionistic. It has the impression that it could vaguely be a part of reality without it being hyperrealistic. It has to achieve a certain level of mystery and intrigue. When seeing her work, you should be able to see what is going on, without immediately
knowing what it truely is.

"The journey of discovering the underlying meaning, is what makes art beautiful." 
- Sarah Lefèvre

Sarahs interest in art began when she was still a young girl. It has always been a big part of my her life. Her grandfather was a sculptor. She used to sneak into his studio to watch him work. He would educate her on how certain things are put together. Then she would take out a notebook and draw. This is a photograph of him doing the same thing with her aunt, Greet Lefèvre, in 1966. She also works in the world of art today. After he passed away, Sarah felt like she owed it to him to keep surrounding herself with creativity. In this way his memory would never fade. 


Sarah is currently studying arts at 'Sint-Lucas', a well known School of Arts in Ghent. Here, she has learned to open her minds to different forms of art. When she was young figuratif work was her only interest. Now, abstract en conceptual art equally entertain her. This, she learned not only from the teachers, but also from all the students. It's extremely usefull to be surrounded by people who have different opinions on the matter. In the years she has been there, her work has evolved in ways she never thought possible.

Sarah inspires to be an painter worthy of the name 'artist'. She tries to expiriment as much as possible with her work. Different colors, different backgrounds, different styles even. There are lot of painters who inspire her to try different ways of painting. In every Art Movement there is at least one artist who can teach you something about the craft. Sarah finds it important to look at their work and try to incorporate their views with what she does.
